Executive Summary

  • The recent HBF MarTech workshop brought together cross-functional leaders across Marketing, Digital, and Member Engagement to align on a unified strategy for first-party data and personalisation.
  • HBF's ambition is to transition from a reactive health insurer to a proactive "health partner" (the HBF30 vision), driving net policy growth through improved East Coast acquisition and Western Australia member retention.
  • By architecting a centralised decisioning engine powered by the Salesforce ecosystem, HBF can move from segment-based "spray and pray" marketing to 1:1 personalisation, establishing a formidable competitive moat.

What We Heard

— Key themes from across the workshop
Theme 01

Data Limitations

The Challenge

There is currently a lack of actionable data at every stage of the user funnels.

In early stage awareness there is a lack of identifying members across channels to optimise paid media spend, which leads to wasted spend and potentially detrimental new offers shown to existing customers.

In quote to conversion — an internal business rule currently dictates that all customer quote data (across branch, call centre, and digital) is purged from the CRM after 30 days. This practice completely destroys the ability to conduct long-term lead nurturing, forcing HBF to waste acquisition budget by constantly remarketing to the exact same prospects months down the line.

In later stage there is a lack of identification of members on web channels despite previous sessions or other device usage logged in.

"We currently have this business rule that blows away all quotes in the CRM after 30 days"

Theme 02

Disconnected Experiences

The Challenge

Current reliance on a custom-built, disparate incentives engine connected via a third-party integration layer (InRule) carries regulatory risk and technical debt.

Because data is not connected seamlessly across systems, current digital personalisation efforts remain isolated, rule-based, and generic (e.g. cookie-level targeting), rather than delivering true 1:1 contextual experiences. Personalisation is not able to operate in real time and deliver a real Health Partner benefit. It's currently inconsistent across channels.

"At the moment, I hate to say it, but it's a lot of spray and prey"

Theme 03

Structural Silos — Technical and Team

The Challenge

HBF's current operating model is divided across core customer deliverables by channel and funnel stage — Digital, CRM, and Marketing teams. Determining who designs & builds audiences and who owns the end-to-end customer journey will expedite decisions. A centralised MarTech steering committee has been proposed as a support mechanism.

A unified architectural decision on where the personalisation decisioning engine sits will provide a single source of truth to support team collaboration.

"The two biggest blocks as to why this hasn't progressed already are just structure and accountability"

HBF Personalisation — Agreed Definition

"Personalisation is how HBF earns every member's confidence by showing up in their everyday health journey so they feel empowered and confident in their health partner."

2. Variations (for consideration)

Option 1 — Trusted Partner

"Showing up consistently"

Personalisation is how HBF supports every member by showing up consistently and with relevance in their everyday lives, so that they always feel seen, valued and confident in their health partner.

Option 2 — Empowerment Through Relevance

"Recognising where they are in life"

Personalisation is how HBF empowers every member by recognising where they are in life and tailoring every interaction to what matters to them, so that they feel in control and confident their health partner is always working for them.

Option 3 — Seamless & Proactive Partnership

"Never feel the need to look elsewhere"

Personalisation is how HBF earns every member's trust by proactively and seamlessly connecting them to the right support, products and moments throughout their lives, so that they never feel the need to look anywhere else.

Awareness
Life Stage Event Prospecting

Turning 31, first child, entering the workforce, or a partner changing employer are high-probability triggers for a new health insurance decision. Build predictive or rules-based audiences around these life stage signals to intercept prospects at the exact moment their need for cover becomes urgent.

Provider Network as a Discovery Signal

An interaction with an HBF-affiliated physio, dental clinic, or GP is a signal of both health engagement and potential consideration. Surface targeted awareness content to uninsured prospects who engage with affiliated providers via co-marketing, directory listings, or digital referrals.

Partner Data Sharing & Clean Rooms

Extend clean room data collaboration to partners such as HBF running events to enable personalised messaging for warm leads — without sharing raw customer data across organisational boundaries.

Workshop Vote
Advertising Exclusions (Suppression of Existing Members)

Sync current CRM members to Google, Meta, and other paid platforms to remove active policyholders from acquisition targeting. Eliminates wasted spend, avoids brand confusion of a member receiving a ‘join HBF’ ad, and redirects media budget toward genuine net new prospects.

Workshop Vote
AI Driven Lookalike Audience Prospecting

Identify the behavioural and demographic profile of HBF’s highest LTV members and build lookalike audiences on paid platforms. For the East Coast, target “good switcher” profiles — prospects likely to retain long-term rather than those who switch funds annually for the best deal.

Personalised Advertising — Geo-Targeted PHI Reform Education

Lifetime Health Cover loading and the April premium review motivate first-time purchasing decisions among younger East Coast prospects. Serve geo-targeted ads explaining PHI implications specific to the prospect’s age, location, and cover status — converting regulatory urgency into a positive brand conversation.

Single-Product Upgrade (NBA) Campaigns

~220,000 HBF members hold a single product only — hospital only or extras only — representing the highest value cross-sell opportunity in the existing base. In-app banners, personalised portal messages, and triggered email sequences surface the most relevant upgrade proposition at the moment of cross-sell intent.

Proactive Behavioural Distress Support

Navigation to pages associated with financial hardship, payment difficulty, or fund switching triggers a proactive member care flag. Reaching the member before they complete a cancellation or switch, and offering hardship support, a cover adjustment, or a payment plan.

Workshop Vote
Referral & Member-Get-Member Campaigns

Identify members with high NPS proxy signals (low contact centre touchpoints, consistent claims utilisation, long tenure) and trigger personalised referral invitations at peak satisfaction moments. Cost per acquisition is structurally lower than paid media; quality of acquired members structurally higher.

Workshop Vote
Policy Health Check Triggers

A missed or late payment and life stage trigger (marriage, divorce, new dependants, dependant removal) are strong predictors of member review. These triggers initiate a personalised policy health check — acknowledging the change, offering a specialist review, and surfacing the most relevant cover adjustment.

Loyalty Recognition & Anniversary Milestones

Long-tenure members receive no acknowledgement of loyalty while competitors actively target them with renewal communications. A personalised loyalty programme — milestone messages, exclusive benefits, priority access to new services — creates an emotional anchor that generic price-based retention offers cannot replicate.

Member Perks Personalisation & Offer Curation

Integrate perks engagement data with the member 360 to surface personalised recommendations — gym claims members see fitness discount offers; members who browsed mental health content see relevant wellbeing perks. Makes the perks programme feel curated rather than generic, increasing both engagement and perceived membership value.

Extras Limit Alerts & Benefit Optimisation

Trigger a personalised alert when a member approaches the annual limit on a frequently used extras benefit. Creates a timely, value-adding touchpoint that reinforces the health partner narrative and drives utilisation or upgrade depending on the member’s position relative to their limit.

Life Event & Dependent Change Journeys

A child turning 25, a student returning home, a marriage — each signals that cover needs are in flux. Detect these changes and trigger a personalised journey confirming the policy is updated correctly, surfacing relevant next steps (family cover options, new-member onboarding for the dependant, household referral opportunities).

App & Push Engagement Personalisation

Use Case 1 Download Triggers & Service Alerts: personalised push campaigns to drive app adoption, then push for claims updates, benefit reminders, and relevant content — increasing engagement frequency without increasing service cost.
Use Case 2 Tailored Claims UX: personalise the claims journey by product type, claim history, and channel preference.

Offboarding & Retention
ID Pre-Cancellation Behaviour Detection

Members researching cancellation on web channels are displaying intent signals to leave. Connect authenticated website session data to the CRM in real time to identify pre-cancellation behaviours and trigger an intervention — via marketing or service channels — before the member reaches the point of action.

Workshop Vote
Cross-Channel Retention Journeys

A cancellation propensity model identifies members who have accumulated churn risk signals — arrears, missed payments, reduced claims activity, competitor brand interactions, and life stage changes. When a member crosses a risk threshold, trigger an automated multi-channel journey: personalised content, value messaging, a save offer, and a direct route to the specialist retention team.

Workshop Vote
Cross-Channel Win-Back Advertising

Use Case 1 Target recently lapsed members via social and paid platforms with personalised creative and offers during the re-engagement window.
Use Case 2 Competitor Awareness Personalisation: where the fund a lapsed member moved to is known — via survey, social listening, or partner data — calibrate win-back communication to specifically address the perceived advantage of the competing fund.

Lapsed Member Segment Analysis

Systematically analyse exit surveys, cancellation reason codes, and behavioural patterns that preceded lapse (life events, tenure, product type, geography, and acquisition channel). Determine whether churn is driven by price sensitivity, product dissatisfaction, life stage exits, or competitive switching — and tailor the re-engagement approach for each segment accordingly.

HBF Transformation Roadmap

— 4 Phases of Strategic Vision

HBF's transformation is structured across four phases, each building on the last to progressively shift from broad acquisition and foundational data capability through to a deeply personalised, loyalty-driven membership experience. Together they form a coherent arc: attract the right members → convert and engage them meaningfully → retain them proactively → reward them in ways that create lasting loyalty.

Phase 1
Acquire & Activate the Right Members

Strategic Intent: Build the data and media infrastructure to attract high-value prospects and convert them more efficiently — with less wasted spend and more precision targeting.

Phase 1 lays the essential groundwork for everything that follows. The focus is on getting HBF in front of the right people at the right moment — whether that's someone experiencing a life stage event, a lapsed member who can be recaptured, or a net-new prospect who looks like HBF's best customers. It also establishes the data hygiene and suppression logic that prevents wasted ad spend on existing members or poor-fit audiences.

Key business capabilities unlocked:

  • Predict life stage moments (new family, ageing parents, new job) to reach prospects at peak insurance intent
  • Build AI-driven lookalike audiences from HBF's best member profiles to fuel East Coast growth
  • Suppress active members and recent joiners from acquisition campaigns — protecting brand experience and media budget
  • Re-engage prospects who started but didn't complete a quote
  • Lay the data foundation for clean room partnerships and co-marketing with provider networks
Salesforce products: Data Cloud · MCE+
Phase 2
Convert & Engage Across Every Channel

Strategic Intent: Create a seamless, personalised experience across web, app, and marketing channels that removes friction from conversion and deepens engagement for existing members.

With acquisition signals flowing, Phase 2 shifts the focus inward — to the experience HBF delivers once a prospect or member is actively engaging. This phase is about eliminating the disjointed, one-size-fits-all experience and replacing it with one that feels genuinely responsive. A prospect visiting the website should see content relevant to their situation. A member who's just made a significant claim should receive tailored support. Someone at risk of cancelling should be reached before they act.

Key business capabilities unlocked:

  • Personalise the HBF website in real time for both known members and anonymous visitors
  • Stitch together behavioural signals across web, app, and email to create a truly continuous member experience
  • Detect pre-cancellation behaviour and trigger rapid, personalised intervention before members leave
  • Connect claims events and life moments (new dependent, policy review) to relevant, timely outreach
  • Drive app adoption and reduce inbound service volume through proactive push personalisation
Salesforce products: Data Cloud · MCE+ · Personalization · Service Cloud
Phase 3
Maximise Value Through Intelligent Product Decisioning

Strategic Intent: Move from campaign-driven cross-sell to always-on, intelligent offer and product recommendation — maximising revenue from HBF's existing member base.

Phase 3 is where the commercial returns compound. HBF's 220,000 single-product members represent the single largest untapped revenue opportunity in the book — and converting even a fraction of them to combined hospital and extras cover requires precision, not volume. This phase introduces the decisioning infrastructure that makes every offer feel like it was made specifically for that member, at exactly the right moment.

Key business capabilities unlocked:

  • Deploy a centralised offer decisioning engine that factors in product eligibility, geography, and propensity to buy
  • Run Next Best Action campaigns targeting single-product members with personalised upgrade pathways
  • Generate life stage–driven product recommendations using claims history, household data, and ML modelling
  • Remove cognitive load at the point of conversion by surfacing one clear, relevant offer rather than a catalogue
Salesforce products: Data Cloud · Personalization · MCE+
Phase 4
Build Loyalty That Can't Be Replicated on Price

Strategic Intent: Transform HBF's membership from a commodity transaction into a valued, emotionally resonant relationship — making loyalty a structural advantage, not just a retention tactic.

Phase 4 completes the arc. The members HBF has acquired, engaged, and grown in value through Phases 1–3 now need a reason to stay that goes beyond price comparison. Phase 4 delivers that — through a loyalty programme that feels personal, perks that reflect individual behaviour and preferences, and recognition moments that create genuine emotional connection. These capabilities are the hardest to copy and the most durable in a commoditised market.

Key business capabilities unlocked:

  • Curate member perks and offers based on individual health behaviours, usage patterns, and life stage — not generic discounts
  • Recognise loyalty milestones (anniversaries, tenure, health achievements) in ways that feel meaningful, not transactional
  • Build a loyalty programme that makes long-tenure members feel seen and valued in ways a cheaper competitor simply cannot replicate
Salesforce products: Loyalty Management · Data Cloud · Personalization · MCE+

How the Phases Connect

The four phases are not independent workstreams — each one creates the conditions for the next. Phase 1 builds the data and audience infrastructure that makes Phase 2 personalisation possible. Phase 2 generates the behavioural and engagement signals that power Phase 3 decisioning. And Phase 3's commercial outcomes — more members on higher-value products — expand the base of engaged members that Phase 4 loyalty turns into advocates.

The result is a transformation that compounds in value over time: each phase delivers standalone returns, but the full strategic vision is realised only when all four work together.
